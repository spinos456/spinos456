Role of a Central Laboratory in Clinical Trials

A central laboratory, as the title indicates, is a laboratory where all test results from clinics that have participated in a trial are sent. Depending on the location of the Central Laboratory in relation to the site, this could cause logistical issues. On the other hand, the Central Laboratory is set up to run all laboratory tests requested by the sponsor and employs validated machinery, standardised methods and reference ranges, and trial-specific testing kits. This ensures that the results and analyses are consistent.
